SNELL: The Mathematician of Refraction

Willebrord Snellius (1580-1626) was a Dutch mathematician and astronomer known for his work in geometry and optics, particularly for Snell's law of refraction.

Early Life

Born in Leiden, Snellius was a child prodigy who later studied at the University of Leiden.

Contributions

His major contributions include the law of refraction which describes how light bends when it enters a different medium.

Legacy

Snell's work laid the foundation for future studies in optics and continues to influence modern physics.
